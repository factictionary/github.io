At Factictionary, we believe powerful tools and quality education shouldn't require sacrificing your privacy. Our mission is to provide comprehensive learning resources and practical tools that respect your data and work entirely in your browser.

Our Story

Factictionary was born from a simple frustration: why do most online tools require you to upload your personal files to their servers? Why should educational content be tied to platforms that track and monetize your behavior?

In 2025, as remote work became the norm and privacy concerns grew louder, we realized there was a gap in the market for truly privacy-first educational tools. We set out to create a platform where you could learn, create, and grow without compromising your data security.

Today, Factictionary serves as a bridge between comprehensive educational content and practical, privacy-first tools. Every feature we build follows our core principle: your data should never leave your device unless you explicitly choose to share it.

Why Privacy Matters to Us

In an age where personal data has become a commodity, we believe the right to privacy isn't just a legal requirement—it's a fundamental human right. Every tool we create and every piece of content we publish respects this principle.

Our Privacy Commitments

  • No data collection beyond essential analytics
  • All file processing happens in your browser
  • No behavioral tracking or profiling
  • No third-party tracking scripts
  • Transparent about any data we do collect
  • Open-source tools where possible

What We Don't Do

  • Sell your data to third parties
  • Profile you for advertising purposes
  • Use cookies for behavioral tracking
  • Store your uploaded files on our servers
  • Share information with advertising networks
  • Require unnecessary personal information
